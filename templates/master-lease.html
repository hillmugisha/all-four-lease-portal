MASTER VEHICLE LEASE AGREEMENT

THIS MASTER VEHICLE LEASE AGREEMENT and any executed addenda (collectively the “Agreement”) is made and entered on {{fmtDate lease.lease_date}} by and between {{lessor.name}} (the “Lessor”), an Iowa Limited Liability Company, located at {{lessor.address}}{{#if lessor.po_box}}, {{lessor.po_box}}{{/if}}, {{lessor.city}}, {{lessor.state}} {{lessor.zip}} and (“Lessee”) {{lessee.name}}{{#if lessee.entity_type}}, a {{lessee.entity_type}}{{/if}}, located at {{lessee.address}}, {{lessee.city}}, {{lessee.state}} {{lessee.zip}}.

Lessor is engaged in the business of leasing motor vehicles (“Vehicles”), and Lessee desires to lease Vehicles from Lessor. This Agreement sets forth the terms and conditions by which Lessor will lease Vehicles to Lessee. The lease terms and conditions for an individual motor vehicle (a “Vehicle”) are set forth in a Vehicle Lease Schedule (“VLS”) and incorporated into this Agreement by reference.

In consideration of the mutual covenants and conditions contained herein, and intending to be legally bound, Lessor and Lessee hereby agree as follows:

1. LEASE OF VEHICLES

Lessor agrees to lease to Lessee the Vehicles set forth in VLSs accepted by Lessor. Lessee may, from time to time, execute and deliver to Lessor one or more vehicle lease schedules (“VLSs”) specifying equipment, accessories, color and other details desired for Vehicles. Upon Lessee’s execution of a VLS, Lessor will order the Vehicle(s) specified in the VLS from vehicle manufacturers. Lessor’s obligation to accept VLSs for any specific number of Vehicles or Lessor’s obligation to supply Vehicles is subject to the availability of such Vehicles. Lessor will use commercially reasonable efforts to obtain Vehicles for which it has accepted VLSs. Lessor shall not be liable to Lessee if the Vehicles specified in VLSs are not available. Acceptance of a Vehicle by Lessee with equipment not specified in a VLS shall constitute acceptance of such additional equipment by Lessee and Lessee agrees to pay for the cost of such additional equipment. Lessee expressly acknowledges that it acquires no right, title or interest in or to the Vehicles described in VLSs, except as a Lessee. As such, Lessee agrees that it shall not attempt any private or public sale of such Vehicles under this Agreement. Lessee additionally acknowledges that the Lessee will not be treated as owner of the property subject to the Master Vehicle Lease Agreement for federal income tax purposes. The creditworthiness of customer and guarantor is a material condition to this agreement. Nothing herein shall be construed to require {{lessor.name}} to agree to lease any vehicle to customer.

2. LEASE TERM AND PAYMENTS

A. Lease Term. The term of lease for each Vehicle shall commence on the date the Vehicle is delivered to Lessee at the location agreed upon between Lessor and Lessee in the VLS (the “Date in Service”). The initial term of the lease shall be eighty four (84) months, at which time, Lessor shall own the Vehicles assuming all payments have been made and all other obligations under the MLS are satisfied. At the end of the lease term, and upon such conditions being met, Lessor shall provide clean title to the Vehicles to Lessee. Notwithstanding the foregoing, in the event the MLA or any individual lease is terminated prior to the end of the term, Lessee shall peaceably deliver to Lessor such Vehicle(s). Any notice of termination by Lessee shall be any time up to thirty (30) days prior to the expiration of the term of the MLA or any individual lease term. Notwithstanding anything in the VLS, in no event shall the lease term be for a period of less than twelve months and one day.

B. Lease Payments. Lessee agrees to pay Lessor in advance by the tenth (10th) day of each full calendar month, the monthly rental specified in each VLS, plus applicable sales tax, use tax and other charges payable by Lessee as specified in each VLS. All payments shall be made to Lessor at the address noted on the invoice or at such other address as Lessor may direct by notice to Lessee. In the event a VLS is terminated on a date other than its billing anniversary date during any year during the term of such VLS, Lessee shall pay to Lessor, upon receipt of an invoice, the difference between the finance charges included in the rental paid since the last previous billing anniversary date for the Vehicle, and the actual finance charges incurred for such period by Lessor relating to such Vehicle. Lessor may assess delinquent charges against Lessee, at the highest rate of interest permitted by law, (but not more than eighteen percent per annum), for any payments not received by the tenth (10th).

C. Prorations. If the Date in Service or the Termination date for a Vehicle occurs other than the first or last day of a month, the monthly rental for such Vehicle shall be prorated for that month. Subject to the notice requirements contained in Section 10 B (Return of Vehicles).

D. Lessee’s Waiver of Defense. Any sums payable by Lessee to Lessor pursuant to this Agreement or any VLS shall not be subject to any defense, setoff or counterclaim which Lessee may have against Lessor, any manufacturer or supplier of the Vehicles for any reason whatsoever.

3. FEES MAINTENANCE: NOTIFICATION

A. Fees and Taxes. All Vehicles shall be registered, licensed, and titled in the name of Lessor. Lessee agrees to pay all costs, fees and expenses required in the licensing, inspection, registration or in obtaining any necessary certificates for the Vehicles, courtesy delivery charges, as well as all taxes, including sales tax, actual tax paid may be less due to credits taken by Lessor, use tax, rental tax, leasing tax, ad valorem tax, excise tax, local tax and personal property taxes (except federal or state income taxes of Lessor) imposed upon a Vehicle during the term of each VLS. Lessor shall have the right to pay any such taxes or fees imposed on any such Vehicles as well as any costs or expenses related to the purchase and lease of the Vehicle and lessee agrees to reimburse Lessor for all such costs, fees, taxes and expenses, including any tax credits utilized by Lessor and further any tax credits available to the Lessor arising from the purchase or as a result of the ultimate disposition of the Vehicle shall be the sole property of the Lessor.

B. Maintenance. Lessee is responsible for the maintenance and repair of each Vehicle and agrees to maintain each Vehicle at its own expense and in compliance with all maintenance procedures required or recommended by the Vehicle’s manufacturer’s warranty and service bulletins. All Vehicles shall be periodically inspected, at Lessee’s expense, as required by law. Lessee agrees that whenever any governmental law, ordinance, regulation or required inspection requires any modification to Vehicles or its equipment, Lessee will comply with such requirement at Lessee’s expense.

C. Dangerous Conditions. In the event Lessor is notified by the manufacturer of a Vehicle that a possible defect or other possible unsafe condition may exist in or any part of such Vehicle, Lessor agrees to mail a copy of such notification to Lessee. Lessee agrees to notify the driver of the Vehicle and waives any right to receive any additional notice from Lessor.

4. USE OF VEHICLES

A. Lessee represents and warrants to Lessor that the Vehicles are leased for use in the United States for business and commercial purposes.

B. Lessee agrees that the Vehicles shall be operated only by licensed drivers over twenty-one (21) years of age and employed by or under the control of Lessee. No driver of a Vehicle shall have the authority to act for or on behalf of Lessor or deemed to be their agent, servant, or employee. Lessee shall not use the Vehicles for transportation of persons or property for hire.

C. Lessee covenants and agrees that the Vehicles shall be used only for lawful purposes and shall be operated in compliance with all applicable laws. Lessee agrees to hold {{lessor.name}} harmless from and against any and all fines, forfeiture, confiscations or penalties assessed against such Vehicles and from any and all damages suffered by Lessor from violations of any statute, law, ordinance, rule or regulation relating thereto. Lessee agrees to pay Lessor a service charge of one hundred twenty-five dollars ($125.00) per notice whenever it is necessary for Lessor to investigate and forward warning or delinquency notices to Lessee for fines, citations or penalties not paid by Lessee.

D. Lessee shall not tamper with or permit any tampering with or adjustment to the odometers of the Vehicles. Lessee agrees to indemnify and hold {{lessor.name}} harmless from and against any and all actions, claims, suits, damages, costs and expenses, including reasonable attorney’s fee caused by or arising from any claimed violations by Lessee, its drivers, agents, servants or employees, or anyone directed by them, of the odometer laws of any state or Federal law, including the Odometer Disclosure Act (Public Law 92-513, Title IV), the Motor Vehicle Information and Cost Savings Act, as amended by the Truth in Mileage Act of 1986 (15 USC S1901 et seq.).

E. Lessor may immediately terminate this Agreement if any Vehicle is used in violation of the provisions of this Section 4 (Use of Vehicles).

F. Lessee represents that more than 50% of the use of any Vehicle subject to the Master Vehicle Lease Agreement will be in its trade or business.

5. INSURANCE

Reserved.

6. Operation of Vehicles Outside the United States.

Lessee agrees to obtain the above coverages and any other coverages required by law in the event a Vehicle is temporarily operated outside the United States and shall be responsible for all loss or damages arising from or occurring during or as a result of operation of a Vehicle outside the United States.

7. Indemnity.

In addition to any other specific indemnities in this Agreement, Lessee assumes liability for and will indemnify, defend, protect, save and hold harmless {{lessor.name}} from and against any and all liabilities, losses, damages, penalties, claims, actions, suits, costs, expenses and disbursements, including court costs and legal expenses, of any kind and nature, imposed on, incurred by or asserted against {{lessor.name}} in any way relating to or arising out of this Agreement, the manufacture, purchase, ownership, delivery, lease, possession, use, operation, condition, repair, return or other disposition of a Vehicle, including, without limitation, any claim alleging latent and other defects whether or not discoverable by {{lessor.name}}, and any other claim arising out of strict liability in tort, whether or not in either instance relating to an event occurring during the term of this Agreement; and any claim for patent or trademark infringement. Lessee agrees to give Lessor and Lessor agrees to give Lessee notice of any claim or liability hereby indemnified against promptly upon learning of such claim or liability.

8. LOSS OR DAMAGE

A. Force Majeure Affecting Delivery. Lessor shall not be liable to Lessee for failure to deliver a Vehicle specified in a VLS if such failure results from strikes, labor difficulties, acts of God or of a public enemy, acts of any Government, fires, floods, epidemic, freight embargoes or any other cause beyond the control of Lessor.

B. Interruption of Service. Lessee’s responsibility for payments of all charges due for each Vehicle under the terms of a VLS and this Agreement shall continue without abatement during the time a Vehicle is confiscated, stolen, converted, destroyed, damaged, or during the time required for any repair, adjustment or servicing of such Vehicle. In the event of irreparable damage to or theft of a Vehicle, all payments shall continue and cease only upon payment to Lessor by or on behalf of Lessee the Depreciated Book Value (as defined in Section 10E (Return of Vehicles)) of the Vehicle as of the date of the loss. Lessor is not responsible for providing temporary Vehicle replacements while Vehicles are out of service for maintenance of repairs, for interruption of service or if a Vehicle is stolen or confiscated.

C. LIABILITY OF LESSOR. LESSOR SHALL NOT BE LIABLE TO LESSEE, ITS OFFICERS, DIRECTORS, BOARD OF REGENTS, EMPLOYEES, AFFILIATES OR SUBSIDIARIES, AGENTS, OR TO ANY PERSON, FOR ANY BUSINESS LOSS, ANY LOSS OF USE, LOSS OF ANY VEHICLE, LOSS OF TIME OR INCONVENIENCE OR INTERRUPTION OF OR DAMAGE TO BUSINESS OR PROFITS, OR FOR OTHER DAMAGES OF ANY KIND OR NATURE, PAID OR INCURRED BY LESSEE CAUSED BY THE CONFISCATION, THEFT, CONVERSION, DESTRUCTION, LOSS, REPAIR, ADJUSTMENT, SERVICING, REPLACEMENT OR ANY INTERRUPTION IN THE OPERATION OR AVAILABILITY FOR ANY REASON OF ANY VEHICLE LEASED UNDER THIS AGREEMENT.

9. TERM OF AGREEMENT.

Subject to the notice provisions contained in Section 10B (Return of Vehicles), the terms and conditions of this Agreement shall continue in full force and effect and shall be binding upon the Lessee until the return of all Vehicles to the custody of Lessor. Lessee’s obligations to self-insure Vehicles shall survive termination of this Agreement.

10. RETURN OF VEHICLES

A. Documents. Unless, the VLS term has expired and Lessee takes possession of the Vehicle(s) via title transfer from Lessor, upon the expiration or termination of the VLS term for each Vehicle, Lessee shall deliver possession of such Vehicle to Lessor at a mutually agreeable location, together with the Vehicle’s license plates, registration certificates, and other similar documents. Lessee shall execute or cause to be executed and delivered to Lessor an odometer disclosure statement in the form supplied by Lessor, disclosing the accurate mileage of the Vehicle in accordance with the Motor Vehicle information and Cost Savings Act, as amended by the Truth in Mileage Act of 1986 (15 USC s1901 et seq.) and the regulations promulgated there under (the “Acts”). Failure to complete the odometer disclosure statement or the making of a false statement on the odometer disclosure statement could result in the imposition of fines and/or imprisonment in accordance with the Acts.

B. Notice of Termination. Lessee shall give Lessor not less than thirty (30) days written notice of the return of any Vehicle. Vehicles shall be returned to the custody of Lessor no later than five (5) days after expiration of the notice period. In the event Lessee gives Lessor less than thirty (30) day notice prior to the return of a Vehicle, Lessee’s obligations pertaining to such Vehicle shall continue through the end of such required thirty (30) day notice period notwithstanding the return of the Vehicle to Lessor.

C. Excess Wear and Tear. Vehicles shall be returned without any physical damage or mechanical malfunctions less reasonable wear and tear. Lessee will be responsible to reimburse Lessor for remedying any unrepaired or unsatisfactorily repaired physical damage or mechanical malfunctions.

D. Net Sales price. The Net Sales Price of a Vehicle is defined as the net proceeds realized by Lessor from the sale of the Vehicle, after deducting all costs and expenses incurred in connection with such sale. Lessee agrees that Lessor may incur such expenses as Lessor deems suitable, in its sole discretion, for reconditioning, transferring to another location, obtaining current license plates, auction or broker’s fees, and such other expenses which may reasonably be incurred in connection with the disposal of Vehicles. All such expenses shall be deducted from the sale proceeds in arriving at the Net Sale Price for the Vehicle.

E. Depreciated Book Value. The depreciated Book Value of a Vehicle is defined as the original capitalized cost of the Vehicle as specified in the VLS, less accumulated depreciation at the rate specified in the VLS, for the number of months the Vehicle was in the possession of Lessee.

F. Disposal of Vehicles. Upon the return of a Vehicle to Lessor, Lessor shall use reasonable efforts to sell such Vehicle for a reasonable wholesale price, as determined by Lessor in its sole discretion. If the Net Sales Price of the Vehicle is greater than its Depreciated Book Value, a credit will be issued to Lessee for such difference, less a $195.00 Disposition Fee. No such credit for a Net Sales Price greater than Depreciated Book Value shall be given Lessee if Lessee is then in default on any payments due Lessor or in any of its obligations under this Agreement. If the Net Sales Price of the Vehicle is less than the Depreciated Book Value of such Vehicle, Lessee will be invoiced for the difference, plus a $195.00 Disposition Fee, and shall pay Lessor such difference upon receipt of the invoice.

11. DEFAULT OF LESSEE.

A. Events of Default. Lessee shall be deemed in default of this Agreement should Lessee default on any payments required to be made by Lessee, default in performance of any other covenant, term or condition of this Agreement, if Lessee or any guarantor of this Agreement files a petition in bankruptcy or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, if proceedings in bankruptcy are instituted against Lessee or any guarantor of this Agreement, Lessee, or any guarantor of this Agreement is adjudicated a bankrupt, if a receiver is appointed for Lessee, or any guarantor of this Agreement, if Lessee permits or suffers any distress, attachment, levy or execution against any of its property.

B. Lessor’s Remedies. In the event of any default by Lessee of its obligations hereunder, Lessor may, at its option, and without notice or demand on Lessee, declare this Agreement and each VLS terminated. Upon termination, Lessee shall remain liable for and shall pay to Lessor all sums due Lessor as of the date of termination and, in addition, monthly rental payments sufficient to satisfy the minimum number of monthly payments required for any terminated VLS. Whether or not such payment is made, Lessor may repossess the Vehicles and obtain and retain all payments previously made and required. Lessor shall have the right to recover the Vehicle(s) without the need for filing any legal action by Lessor Against Lessee for replevin of return of vehicles to Lessor. In the event of default, Lessee consents and agrees that Lessor may enter upon property of Lessee to recover Vehicle(s). In the event of default, Lessor may recover possession of Vehicle(s) wherever the Vehicle(s) may be found so long as the recovery of the Vehicle(s) does not constitute a breach of the peace or violate the property rights of third parties. The proceeds of disposition of the Vehicles shall be distributed as required by Section 10 F (Return of Vehicles). Any repossession or resale of the Vehicles shall not bar an action for a deficiency, and the bringing of an action for any sums owed, with an entry of judgment against Lessee, shall not bar Lessor’s right to repossess the Vehicle(s). The rights and remedies of Lessor are in addition to those provided by law. Lessor shall have no obligation to exercise any such remedy, and the exercise of any remedy shall not release Lessee from any of its obligations. Lessee shall pay Lessor on demand all expenses of collection and repossession, including court costs and reasonable attorney’s fees incurred by Lessor in pursuing claims against Lessee.

12. DISCLAIMER OF WARRANTY

EXCEPT AS OTHERWISE EXPRESSLY PROVIDED BY THIS AGREEMENT, NEITHER {{lessor.name}} NOR LESSOR MAKE ANY EXPRESS OR IMPLIED WARRANTY AS TO ANY MATTER WHATSOEVER, INCLUDING, WITHOUT LIMITATION, THE CONDITION OF THE VEHICLES OR THEIR MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR ANY PARTICULAR PURPOSE. NO DEFECT OF THE VEHICLES SHALL RELIEVE LESSEE OF ANY OF ITS OBLIGATIONS UNDER THIS AGREEMENT. THE ONLY WARRANTY AVAILABLE TO LESSOR IS THAT OF THE ORIGINAL EQUIPMENT MANUFACTURER.

13. SECURITY INTEREST

{{lessor.name}} or Lessor may grant a security interest in one or more of the Vehicles or VLSs and all sums due hereunder or there under to one or more lending institutions (the “Lenders”). All right, title and interest of {{lessor.name}} or Lessor to the extent assigned shall inure to the benefit of such Lenders, their successor and assigns. Lessee will not assert against such Lenders any defense, set-off or counterclaim with respect to the obligations of Lessee hereunder, including Lessee’s obligation for payments and other amounts due hereunder or under any VLSs notwithstanding any claim Lessee may have against Lessor. Lessee may assert such claims only in an independent action against Lessor. Lessee will execute and acknowledge, upon notice from Lessor, such financing statements and other instruments as may, in the opinion of Lessor, be desirable to give effect to this Section 13, or to record and protect Lessor’s title against any who might claim such title through Lessee. Lessee will not suffer or permit a lien to attach to this Agreement or any Vehicle, whether for repairs to the Vehicle or otherwise.

14. GENERAL

A. Amendment. This Agreement and any duly executed VLSs and other amendments and addenda that may be executed from time to time referencing this Agreement may be modified only in a writing duly executed by both parties.

B. Headings: Construction. The titles of the various sections in this Agreement are intended to facilitate reference only and shall not be employed in construction of any provision of this Agreement. The words “Lessee,” “its,” or “their,” shall be construed to mean a singular, plural, masculine, feminine or neuter, as the case may be.

C. Waiver. The failure of either party to insist upon the performance of any term or condition of this Agreement, or to exercise any right or privilege conferred by this Agreement, shall not constitute a waiver by either party of any such term or condition.

D. Severability. Should any part, term or provision of this Agreement be decided by any court to be illegal, unenforceable or in conflict with any applicable law, the validity of the remaining portions, terms, or provisions of this Agreement shall not be affected.

E. Benefit. This Agreement shall be binding upon the parties, their respective successors, legal representatives, and assigns.

F. Lessee’s Rights. It is understood and agreed that in the event Lessor fails or is unable to perform its obligations hereunder, or in the event Lessor’s interest in any Vehicle shall be levied upon, taken in execution or subjected to encumbrances other than to Lenders, or in the event Lessor becomes insolvent or bankrupt or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors or consents to the appointment of a trustee or receiver, or a trustee or receiver is appointed for Lessor for a substantial portion of its property without its consent, or bankruptcy, reorganization, arrangement, insolvency or liquidation proceedings are instituted by or against Lessor, then in any such event, Lessee shall have the option to terminate this Agreement and to purchase any or all Vehicles leased hereunder on payment of all payments due to the date thereof plus the Depreciated Book Value of the Vehicles purchased or, notwithstanding such default or insolvency, to continue this Agreement in full force and effect.

G. Governing Law: Venue. This Agreement shall be deemed to have been made in and shall be construed in accordance with the laws of the Venue for any action related to this Agreement shall be in an appropriate court in any county selected by Lessor. Lessee hereby agrees to submit to such jurisdiction and waives any and all defenses of lack of jurisdiction.

H. Notices. Any notice required or permitted to be given hereunder shall be deemed to have been given only if in writing and delivered by personal delivery, or forwarded, charges fully prepaid, by Federal Express or any other similar overnight delivery service, or United States certified or registered mail, return receipt requested, addressed to the party to be notified at the address appearing at the head of this Agreement or to such other address as the party to be notified may have, by ten (10) days prior written notice, so specified. All notices shall be deemed given when personally delivered or so forwarded.

I. Effective Date. This Agreement shall not become effective and binding upon the parties hereto until accepted and executed by an officer of Lessor.

J. Entire Agreement. The terms and conditions attached to this MLA and any VLSs shall constitute the entire agreement between Lessor and Lessee with respect to the subject matter hereof and shall supersede and render void any and all terms and conditions contained in Lessee’s purchase order(s) or other ordering documents, regardless of when such purchase order(s) or other ordering documents were or are issued. No terms or conditions set forth in any purchase order(s) or other ordering documents issued by Lessee shall modify, amend, or otherwise alter the terms and conditions of this Agreement, regardless of whether such purchase order(s) or other ordering documents are acknowledged or accepted by Lessor. Lessee acknowledges and agrees that by executing this MLA or submitting any purchase order(s) or other ordering documents in connection with this Agreement, Lessee accepts the terms and conditions of this Agreement as controlling and binding, notwithstanding any contrary provisions in Lessee’s purchase order(s) or other ordering documents.

K. Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Lessor and Lessee may provide the other with a fully executed copy of this Agreement by electronic means, and Lessor and Lessee agree that an executed Agreement received from the other electronically shall be deemed the original, executed Agreement of the transmitting party.
